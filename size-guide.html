How to Measure Your Feet

Follow these simple steps for accurate measurements

1

Prepare Your Materials

Get a ruler or measuring tape, a piece of paper larger than your foot, and a pen or pencil.

2

Trace Your Foot

Place the paper on a hard floor and stand on it. Trace around your foot, keeping the pen vertical.

3

Measure Length

Measure from the heel to the longest toe. This is your foot length in centimeters.

4

Measure Width

Measure the widest part of your foot across the ball. This determines your width size.

Pro Tips

  • Measure in the evening when feet are slightly swollen
  • Measure both feet and use the larger measurement
  • Wear the socks you'll typically use with the shoes
  • Stand up while measuring for accuracy

Perfect Fit Tips

Expert advice for the best shoe fitting experience

Best Time to Shop

Shop for shoes in the afternoon or evening when your feet are naturally slightly larger due to daily activity.

Consider Your Socks

Always consider the thickness of socks you'll wear. Athletic socks require more room than dress socks.

Test the Fit

Walk around to ensure comfort. There should be about a thumb's width of space between your longest toe and the shoe front.

Both Feet Matter

Always measure and fit both feet. Most people have one foot slightly larger than the other.
